## 1. Brand Voice  


### Personality Attributes  


- **Inclusive:** Every body, every fitness level, every background belongs at MoveAtlas.  
- **Dynamic:** We celebrate movement, exploration, and continuous discovery.  
- **Empowering:** We remove friction so users feel in control of their fitness journey.  
- **Transparent:** Clear pricing, honest communication, no hidden conditions.  
- **Tech-Forward but Human:** Seamless infrastructure with community at its core.  


### Voice Characteristics  


| Dimension | We Are | We Are Not |
|------------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Formality | Professional yet relaxed | Corporate or stiff |
| Complexity | Clear and accessible | Jargon-heavy or dumbed down |
| Emotion | Warm and motivating | Aggressive or hype-driven |
| Authority | Confident and informed | Arrogant or vague |
| Humor | Light when appropriate | Forced or sarcastic |

## 2. Tone Guidelines  


### Tone by Context  


| Context | Tone | Example |
|----------|---------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Educational content | Encouraging, patient, clear | "Here’s how to explore new studios near you." |
| Product announcements | Excited, confident, benefit-focused | "We’re expanding to two new cities — your membership just got bigger." |
| Problem-solving | Empathetic, solution-oriented, calm | "We understand the issue. Here’s how we’re fixing it." |
| Thought leadership | Authoritative, forward-looking, insightful | "Urban fitness is evolving — flexibility is the new standard." |
| Social media | Conversational, engaging, concise | "Europe is your gym. Where are you training today?" |
| Partner communication | Professional, business-focused | "Fill your classes with reliable monthly members." |
| Crisis communication | Transparent, accountable, measured | "We want to address this directly and clearly." |

## 3. Key Messaging Framework  


### Brand Promise  


> We make movement frictionless — one membership, unlimited access across Europe.


### Value Propositions  


1. **Unlimited Flexibility:** Access 1000+ studios across 15+ European cities with one membership.  
2. **No Commitment:** Cancel anytime. No long-term contracts.  
3. **Seamless Experience:** Real-time booking through one intuitive app.  
4. **Community Growth:** We grow with our studio partners — not at their expense.  


### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)  


> MoveAtlas is Europe’s lifestyle and sports membership platform connecting fitness enthusiasts with 1000+ studios across 15+ cities through one seamless app. Unlike traditional gyms that limit you to one venue, MoveAtlas gives you the freedom to explore yoga, boxing, climbing, pilates, and more — all under one flexible membership. Europe is your gym.


### Tagline/Slogan  


- Primary: "The World Is Your Gym™"  
- Secondary: "One Membership. Infinite Movement."

## 4. Target Audience Personas  


### Primary Audience  


- **Who:** Urban professionals (25–50) in major cities  
- **Pain Points:** Gym boredom, rigid contracts, limited flexibility, city-to-city travel  
- **Goals:** Stay active, explore new sports, fit workouts into busy schedules  
- **How We Help:** One app, real-time booking, cross-city access, no commitment  
- **Language They Use:** Flexible, discover, unlimited, cancel anytime, variety  


### Secondary Audience  


- **Who:** Boutique fitness studios and gym owners  
- **Pain Points:** Empty class spots, marketing costs, customer churn  
- **Goals:** Reliable revenue, higher occupancy, sustainable growth  
- **How We Help:** Qualified monthly members, real-time booking system, predictable revenue  
- **Language They Use:** Occupancy rate, retention, recurring revenue, growth

## 5. Content Standards  


### Writing Principles  


1. **Clarity first:** If a sentence can be misunderstood, rewrite it  
2. **Lead with value:** Always answer "Why should I care?"  
3. **Be specific:** Use concrete numbers and examples  
4. **Active voice:** Prefer direct, strong phrasing  
5. **Scannable format:** Headers, bullets, short paragraphs  


### Formatting Rules  


- **Headlines:** Title case, under 70 characters, action-oriented  
- **Paragraphs:** Maximum 3–4 sentences  
- **Sentences:** 15–20 words average  
- **CTAs:** Clear and benefit-driven (e.g., "Start exploring today")  


### Grammar & Style  


- Use Oxford comma  
- Spell out one through nine, use digits for 10+  
- Dates: DD, Month, YYYY  


---


## 6. Do's and Don'ts  


### Do's  


- Use inclusive, accessible language  
- Emphasize flexibility and discovery  
- Be transparent about pricing   
- Address the reader directly ("you")  
- Maintain consistency across platforms  


### Don'ts  


- Use corporate jargon  
- Gatekeep fitness culture  
- Overpromise without evidence   
- Sacrifice clarity for creativity

## 7. Channel-Specific Guidelines  


### Blog Posts  


- Length: 800–2000 words  
- Structure: Hook → Context → Value → CTA  
- Focus: Lifestyle - and fitness trends, flexibility, community stories  
- Include internal and relevant external links
